Rillette

by Helen Rennie

Watch the video here!

Ingredients

  • 3433g bonless pork shoulder
  • 27g salt (0.8%)
  • 10g sugar (0.3%)
  • 10g smoke paprika (0.3%)
  • 5g black pepper (0.15%)
  • 40g whole garlic cloves (1.1%)
  • 180g shallots, sliced (5%)
  • a small bunch of fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 84g olive oil (2.5%)

Steps

  1. Remove the fat cap from the pork and score it in a criss-cross pattern. Cut up the pork into 2 inch cubes, scoring any large chunks of fat. In a small bowl, mix the salt, sugar, pimenton, and pepper. Put the pork, fat cap, garlic, and shallots into a large bowl. Sprinkle all over with the salt mixture and mix well. Cover and let sit in the fridge overnight or up to 3 days.
  2. Preheat the oven to 300F (150C) with a rack in the lower third.
  3. Put a little olive oil into an oven safe pot (like a dutch oven). Arrange a layer of pork cubes fat side down. Add the remaining pork with all the aromatics and cover with the fat cap (if you donâ€™t have a fat cap, arrange the top layer of pork fat side up). Cover the pot and bake for 4 hours or until the pork is fork tender.
  4. Cool the pork in the fat until lukewarm. Strain reserving all the liquids and removing the bay leaves and thyme stems. Wait for the fat to rise to the top (about 5 min) and spoon off the fat and reserve. Store the pork, the juice, and the fat in the fridge for up to a week or freeze.
  5. Right after straining the pork, put as much meat as you want into a mixer bowl (in the video, I used 500g). Add 10% of the pork weight in juice (50g juice), and 10% of the pork weight in fat (50g fat). Whip with a paddle attachment until fluffy. Stuff into jars. Smooth the top. Top with fat. Refrigerate overnight before serving. Can be stored in the fridge for up to 2 weeks or frozen.
